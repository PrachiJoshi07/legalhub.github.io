Parsi law is the system of law that governs the Parsi Zoroastrian community in India. It is based on the religious and cultural traditions of Zoroastrianism, and is one of the oldest legal systems in the world.

Parsi law covers a wide range of topics, including marriage, divorce, child custody, maintenance, inheritance, and religious matters. It is derived from a variety of sources, including the Avesta (the holy book of Zoroastrianism), the Vendidad (a commentary on the Avesta), and the customary laws of the Parsi community.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Key features of Parsi law

 

Some of the key features of Parsi law include:

 

  • Equality of men and women: Parsi law is one of the few legal systems in the world that grants equal rights to men and women in all matters of marriage, divorce, child custody, and inheritance.
  • Religious freedom: Parsi law protects the religious freedom of the Parsi community. For example, it prohibits religious conversion and interfaith marriage.
  • Community autonomy: Parsi law grants the Parsi community a great deal of autonomy to manage its own affairs. For example, the Parsi community has its own courts and religious councils.

Application of Parsi law

 

Parsi law applies to all Parsis, regardless of their nationality or place of residence. It is also applied to non-Parsis who marry into the Parsi community.

 

Here are some specific examples of Parsi law:

 

  • Marriage: Parsi law requires that both parties to a marriage be consenting adults. The marriage must also be solemnized by a Parsi priest.

  • Divorce: Parsi law allows for divorce on a number of grounds, including cruelty, desertion, and adultery.

  • Child custody: Parsi law generally awards custody of children to the mother after divorce. However, the father may be granted custody in certain cases, such as if the mother is unfit to care for the children.

  • Maintenance: Parsi law requires the husband to provide maintenance to his wife and children after divorce.

  • Inheritance: Parsi law grants equal inheritance rights to men and women.
