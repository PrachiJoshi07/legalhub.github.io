The Parsi Succession Act, 1865, is a legal enactment that governs the inheritance and succession rights among Parsis, who are followers of the Zoroastrian faith. This act is specific to the Parsi community in India and outlines the rules and procedures for the devolution of property and assets within the Parsi community. Here are the key aspects of the Parsi Succession Act, 1865:

 

Objective

 

  • The primary objective is to provide a legal framework for the inheritance and succession of property among Parsis.
  •  
  • To ensure that Parsi customs and traditions related to succession are upheld.
  •  
  • To protect the rights of legal heirs and beneficiaries within the Parsi community.

Key Provisions

 

  • The Devolution of Property:

 

  • The act specifies the rules for the distribution of the property and assets of a deceased Parsi individual.
  • It outlines the order of succession, defining the legal heirs and their priority in inheriting the property.

 

  • Male and Female Heirs:

 

  • The act distinguishes between male and female heirs and defines their respective rights to inherit.
  • Sons, daughters, widows, and other relatives have specified rights to the deceased's property.

 

  • Intestate Succession:

 

  • In cases where a Parsi individual dies without leaving a valid will (intestate), the act provides rules for the distribution of the property.
  • It establishes a hierarchy of legal heirs who are entitled to inherit based on their relationship to the deceased.

 

  • Wills:

 

  • The act addresses the creation and execution of wills by Parsis.
  • It sets out the formalities and requirements for a will to be considered valid and enforceable within the Parsi community.

 

  • Void Bequests:

 

  • Certain bequests (gifts or legacies) made by Parsis may be declared void if they contravene Parsi customs or religious beliefs.
  • The act specifies circumstances under which such bequests are not legally valid.

 

  • Administration of Estates:

 

  • The act allows for the appointment of administrators when necessary to oversee the distribution of the deceased's property.
  • Administrators are appointed by legal authorities to ensure that the property is distributed according to the act's provisions.

 

Procedures

 

  • The Parsi Succession Act, 1865, primarily defines the rules and principles of succession and inheritance among Parsis.

 

  • Detailed procedures for administering estates and executing the act's provisions are typically carried out through legal processes and Indian courts.

 

Punishments

 

  • The The act itself does not prescribe punitive measures within its provisions.
  • Violations or disputes related to the act may be subject to legal actions and remedies within the Indian legal system, rather than punitive measures defined in the act.

 

Application:

 

  • The Parsi Succession Act applies specifically to Parsi individuals in matters of succession and inheritance.

 

  • It operates alongside other Indian laws and regulations, including general civil laws.
