The primary source of Christian law in India is the Indian Christian Marriage Act, 1872. The Act provides for monogamous marriages between Christians, and allows for divorce on a number of grounds, including cruelty, desertion, and adultery. The Act also provides for child custody and maintenance after divorce.

 

In addition to the Indian Christian Marriage Act, 1872, Christian law in India is also influenced by the Bible and the teachings of the Christian church.
