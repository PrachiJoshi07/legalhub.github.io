The Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act of 1936 is a specific piece of legislation in India that governs marriage and divorce among Parsis, who are followers of the Zoroastrian faith. This act is designed to provide a legal framework for Parsi marriages and divorce proceedings, ensuring that these events are carried out in accordance with Parsi customs and traditions while also addressing legal issues and protections. Here are the key aspects of the Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act of 1936:

 

Objective

 

The primary objectives of the Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act of 1936 are as follows:

 

  • To provide a legal framework for the solemnization and registration of Parsi marriages.

 

  • To regulate Parsi divorce proceedings, ensuring that they follow prescribed legal procedures.

 

  • To protect the rights and interests of Parsi individuals, particularly in matters related to marriage, divorce, and maintenance.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Key Provisions

 

Marriage:

 

  • The act specifies the requirements for the solemnization of Parsi marriages, including the age of marriage and the need for free and voluntary consent.

 

  • It outlines the role of Parsi priests in conducting marriage ceremonies.

 

  • The act allows for the registration of Parsi marriages, and it is compulsory in some cases.

 

Divorce:

 

  • The act provides for the grounds on which a Parsi marriage can be dissolved, including adultery, cruelty, desertion, and more.

 

  • It outlines the procedures for filing divorce petitions and conducting divorce proceedings.

 

  • The act also addresses issues related to alimony and maintenance for the divorced spouse.

 

Procedures

 

Marriage:

 

  • The act requires Parsi marriages to be conducted by Parsi priests or dasturs in the presence of Parsi witnesses.

 

  • Marriage certificates may be issued upon request and registration of the marriage.

 

Divorce:

 

  • In case of divorce, either party may file a petition before the Parsi Matrimonial Court.

 

  • The court may inquire into the grounds for divorce and consider reconciliation efforts.

 

  • After due process, the court may grant a decree of divorce if the grounds are proven.

 

Punishments

 

The Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act of 1936 does not prescribe specific punitive measures within the act itself. However, it is important to note that violation of the act's provisions, such as non-compliance with the legal procedures for marriage and divorce, may result in legal consequences, including the nullification of a marriage or loss of rights in divorce proceedings.

Additionally, as with other personal laws in India, civil courts may also be involved in resolving disputes and enforcing the provisions of the Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act.

It's essential for Parsi individuals seeking marriage or divorce under this act to consult with legal experts who are well-versed in Parsi personal law and Indian legal procedures to ensure their rights are protected and the processes are followed correctly.
